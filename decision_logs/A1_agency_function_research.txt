0. Why this exists

Before writing a single agent, I needed to understand what agencies actually do.
The assignment said "recreate Reclamebureau Eva." Before copying a structure,
I needed to understand what the structure was for. Six questions drove the research.


1. What do agencies produce?

ironpaper.com breakdown of deliverables:
- Strategy: market research, positioning, messaging hierarchy
- Campaign creative: ad copy, visual concepts, brand voice documentation
- Digital execution: social content, email sequences, landing pages
- Analytics and reporting: performance dashboards, A/B results, attribution models
- Sales enablement: pitch decks, product one-pagers, case studies

Even the smallest independent agency produces at least the first three.
Analytics and sales enablement are typically added at scale.


2. Who is involved?

setup.us team structure breakdown:
- Account management: client liaison, briefing, project coordination
- Creative: copywriting, art direction, concept development
- Media: channel strategy, placement, budget allocation
- Technology: web/app builds, automation, tracking infrastructure
- Operations: billing, legal review, compliance, reporting

These are roles, not people. A five-person agency has one person doing
account + operations and one doing media + technology. The functions
still exist — they are just compressed into fewer people.


3. Degree of contribution by phase

Research from ironpaper.com on who leads each campaign phase:

  Discovery/strategy   → Account + Planning (100% lead)
  Brief development    → Account + Creative (collaborative)
  Production           → Creative + Technology (100% lead)
  Distribution         → Media (100% lead)
  Measurement          → Analytics + Account (reporting to client)

No single role owns the full pipeline. The phases require genuinely
different expertise. A creative director makes bad media buying decisions.
A media buyer makes weak copy decisions.


4. Skill overlaps across roles

Three skills span multiple roles:
- Communication + storytelling: account management, creative, new business
- Strategic analysis: account, media, analytics
- Project management: account, operations, traffic management

These overlaps are where coordination happens. Account management is the
hub — it holds context about client goals and distributes it to each specialist.


5. What customers do agencies serve?

B2B agencies (ironpaper.com focus):
- Buying committees of 6–10 people, not single decision-makers
- Sales cycles of 3–18 months
- Content must address different stages of awareness simultaneously

B2C agencies (setup.us focus):
- Direct consumer purchase intent
- Shorter attention span, higher volume creative turnover
- Channel-specific format requirements (TikTok vs. Meta vs. email)

The product being marketed determines the agency structure. B2B = longer
copy, more stakeholders, more rational appeals. B2C = shorter copy,
emotional hooks, platform-native formats.


6. Who is the audience for agency output?

Primary audiences, ranked by frequency:
1. End consumers (ads, social content)
2. Internal sales teams (enablement materials)
3. Buying committees (B2B thought leadership, whitepapers)
4. Journalists and media (PR, press releases)

The same campaign often needs all four. A product launch has consumer ads,
a sales deck, a press release, and a trade media pitch. Agencies coordinate
across all four without the client managing four separate vendors.


Key finding

Even the smallest agency performs exactly five distinct functions:
  (1) product/market analysis
  (2) audience definition
  (3) copy and creative generation
  (4) CTA and conversion optimization
  (5) compliance and legal review

This is not coincidence — these five functions exist because they require
genuinely different context and judgment. A copywriter who also does
compliance misses flags because they are too close to the copy they wrote.
A strategist who also writes copy loses the audience-first framing.

This 1:1 mapping became the agent decomposition for the base pipeline:
vision.py → audience.py → copywriter.py → guardrails.py → cta_optimizer.py
